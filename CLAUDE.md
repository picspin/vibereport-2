# 4. Proposal Design Principles

The agent must follow these principles.

---

## Product First

The proposal must clearly answer:

**Which radiology product becomes stronger because of this research?**

Examples:

* CT scanner feature improvement
* radiation management software capabilities
* AI imaging module development

---

## Clinical Impact

The proposal must address a **real clinical pain point**, such as:

* radiologist workload
* delayed diagnosis
* protocol variability
* radiation exposure concerns

---

## Demonstration Value

The project should create a **showcase use case** that can be replicated globally.

Examples:

* AI-assisted abdominal CT center
* ultra-low-dose CT program
* high-throughput CT workflow

---

## Commercial Relevance

The proposal must highlight:

* market size
* hospital demand
* differentiation from competitors

---

# 5. Proposal Structure

All proposals must follow the structure below.

---

# 5.1 Executive Summary

Summarize:

* clinical challenge
* proposed solution
* product relevance
* expected impact

Length: 150–200 words.

---

# 5.2 Clinical & Workflow Problem

Describe the real-world problem.

Include:

* clinical burden
* imaging workflow inefficiencies
* unmet needs in abdominal radiology

Example:

High CT volume with inconsistent protocol selection leads to unnecessary radiation exposure and workflow inefficiencies.

---

# 5.3 Opportunity for Product Innovation

Explain how the problem connects to **radiology products**.

Examples:

* CT scanner protocol intelligence
* radiation management platforms
* AI-based imaging workflow automation

Clearly define:

**Which product area benefits from this research.**

---

# 5.4 Proposed Scientific Collaboration

Describe the collaboration.

Include:

* clinical partner institution
* study design
* imaging datasets
* workflow observations

Examples:

* multi-center CT workflow study
* AI-assisted abdominal CT evaluation
* radiation analytics pilot program

---

# 5.5 Solution Concept

Define the proposed solution.

Examples:

* AI-assisted CT protocol recommendation
* automated radiation dose monitoring
* abdominal lesion detection AI

Describe:

* system architecture
* workflow integration
* user interaction

---

# 5.6 Product Development Impact

Explain how the research informs **product development**.

Possible outcomes:

* new product features
* improved algorithms
* better workflow integration
* new clinical modules

---

# 5.7 Demonstration Site Strategy

Explain how the partner institution becomes a **reference center**.

Possible positioning:

* Abdominal Imaging Innovation Center
* Low Dose CT Excellence Program
* Intelligent CT Workflow Demonstration Site

Outputs may include:

* clinical publications
* conference presentations
* training programs
* global site visits

---

# 5.8 Validation Plan

Describe evaluation methods.

Metrics may include:

* diagnostic accuracy
* workflow efficiency
* radiation dose reduction
* radiologist productivity

---

# 5.9 Commercial Potential

Discuss:

### Market Demand

* CT imaging growth
* abdominal imaging volume
* radiation safety awareness

### Competitive Landscape

Compare with:

* other imaging vendors
* radiology AI companies

### Scaling Strategy

Explain how the solution could scale across hospitals globally.

---

# 5.10 Implementation Roadmap

Define phases.

### Phase 1 — Concept Study

Data analysis and prototype testing.

### Phase 2 — Clinical Pilot

Workflow integration in partner hospital.

### Phase 3 — Product Integration

Incorporation into commercial product portfolio.
